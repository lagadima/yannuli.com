User research to locate problems

I started with collecting 150 pieces of negative feedback from both the post portal and app store reviews. The data were immense, ecletic and chaotic so that I categorized the major problems with an Elito.

To map the iteration clearly, I used severity rating to locate the most serious problems.

With an overall idea of the problems in head, I conducted four usability testings to gain the first-hand user scenario. In each test, I asked the users to speak aloud their minds and reasons of their meticulous decisions including gesture choices. During the test, I carefully avoided giving out instructions or directly answering questions, instead, I asked for users' motivations, habits, and past experience to guide them through some puzzling point. I made a detailed record of both the comments and the experience, finding that unusual gestures without instructions, ambiguous guides and implications in Folding Fan made users at loss. In one month, I collected four usability test report and started to synthesize the information.

Users' difficulties and comments were again messy so I also used severity rating to summarize them. I distilled three insights from all the data, and identified major problems.

Findings

To encompass the design process and validate my design with my teammates, I refined three insights from the data I gathered.

Now it is time to solve the problems.

Brainstorming and validating solutions

After presenting the insights I found and the problems I identify to my teammates, we meet up to brainstorm the solutions. We discussed along the sitemap and I collected 27 ideas in total regarding every meticulous problem.

After testing the viability and primaries of the ideas, I narrow down the ideas, which will be presented in Folding fan 1.2 released in December.
